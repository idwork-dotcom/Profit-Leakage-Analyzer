# Executive Summary: Profit Leakage & Unit Economics

## 1. Business Context & Objective
Gross Top-Line Revenue and nominal Cost of Goods Sold (COGS) paint an incomplete picture of portfolio health. When factoring in friction costs—payment gateway fees, volatile logistics expenditures, excessive discounting, high return rates, and Customer Acquisition Cost (CAC)—the actual unit economics of an e-commerce sale often degrade significantly.

**The Objective:** Transform a batch of disparate data fragments into an operationalized data pipeline that diagnoses exact profit leakage to the order item-level, delivering True Unit Economics (Contribution Margin and Net Profit).

## 2. Technical Implementation
A Medallion pipeline architecture was built atop PostgreSQL to handle data at scale:
* **Raw:** Data strictly mirrored from source files without transformation.
* **Staging:** Data cleaning, type-casting, filtering, and initial logic derivation (estimating COGS, parsing marketing spend).
* **Analytics Layer:** A robust fact table (`fact_order_items`) serving as the source of truth, flanked by reporting roll-ups designed for instantaneous dashboard querying.
* **Presentation:** A Streamlit BI application deployed as a clean, decision-support dashboard for executives.

### Methodological Assumptions:
1. **CAC Capitalization:** 100% of the channel acquisition cost is applied instantly against the customer's *First Order*. Repeat orders accrue $0 marketing cost, surfacing genuine Lifetime Value (LTV).
2. **Order-Level Cost Allocation:** Gateways are billed at the total transaction level, and discounts are applied cart-wide. To dissect profitability by *item*, these structural costs are systematically allocated downward according to the item's revenue share of the order.
3. **Return Write-offs:** A standard return penalizes the bottom line by incurring a 100% reverse-leg logistics cost matching the forward freight, plus an estimated 10% restocking penalty assigned against the COGS.

## 3. Key Findings & Recommendations

### A. The "Vampire" Categories
While "Beauty" products appear mathematically viable on a gross basis, the excessive return rate (95%+) combined with standard logistics profiles absolutely obliterates its margin. Every order shipped is mathematically destructive under the current operational cadence.
* **Recommendation:** Consider immediate delisting, significantly raising minimum pricing, or passing reverse shipping fees onto the customer for this specific category.

### B. Severe Acquisition Burn on Paid Channels
"Paid Search" carries an aggressive acquisition spend with a CAC that outweighs the immediate first-order margin generated by these cohorts. The channel yields an LTV/CAC ratio of significantly `< 1.0`.
* **Recommendation:** Divert budget heavily toward "Organic", "Referral", and "Email" pipelines which operate efficiently at `> 5.0x` LTV to CAC. "Paid Search" requires drastic retargeting to raise initial Average Order Values (AOV) to compensate for the click costs.

### C. Discount Destruction
Excessive discounting was identified as the secondary largest factor driving "Severe" order losses (defined as >50% net loss per order). Orders utilizing heavy discounts often lack the contribution margin volume to survive standard logistics and gateway fee subtractions.
* **Recommendation:** Shift from percentage-based discounting to threshold-based discounting (e.g., "$10 off orders over $150") to guarantee sufficient gross volume before profit deductions apply.
